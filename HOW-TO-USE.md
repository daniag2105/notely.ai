# How to use Notely.ai

Notely.ai turns a lecture into neat study notes. You give it two things:

- your **slides**, and
- the **transcript** — the written text of what the lecturer said (from Echo360, Teams, Otter, a
  caption file, etc.).

It writes the notes for you, and can drop them straight into Notion. Here's how, step by step.

---

## 1. Make your account (once)

Open the app. A little box pops up. Click **Create an account**, type your email and a password (at
least 8 characters), and click **Create account**.

✅ Your first **5 notes are free**.

## 2. Say what the lecture is

At the top of the app:

- **Unit** — pick your subject/course (e.g. `ENSC3004`).
- **Topic** — type what this lecture was about (e.g. `Week 5 – Heat Transfer`).

This just gives the notes a title and, if you use Notion, sends them to the right page.

## 3. Add your slides

Find the **Lecture slides** box:

- Have a PDF? Click **Upload** and choose the file.
- Only have text? Click **Paste** and paste it in.

## 4. Add the transcript

Find the **Lecture transcript** box, click **Paste**, and paste the transcript in (or **Upload** a
`.txt`, `.vtt`, or `.srt` file). Don't worry about the timestamps — Notely ignores them.

## 5. Choose what you want

- **Notes** – full study notes (the normal choice).
- **Math** – just the formulas and key quantities.
- **Examples** – just the worked examples, solved step by step.

Then pick the detail level (**Concise / Standard / Detailed**). You can also turn on **figures** to
pull diagrams out of your slides and include them.

## 6. Press Generate

Click **Generate** and wait a little. The notes appear on the right as they're written.

## 7. Read them over

Check the notes on the right. That's your study sheet.

## 8. Save or send them

- **Send to Notion** – puts the notes into your Notion, under the right unit/topic. *(Connect Notion
  first — see below.)*
- **Copy** – copies the notes so you can paste them anywhere.
- **Download** – saves them as a file.

---

## Want your notes in Notion? (optional, one-time)

Open **Settings ▸ Connect Notion**. Your browser opens; tick the pages Notely is allowed to use;
done. From then on, **Send to Notion** just works. (No token to copy — it's already set up.)

## When your 5 free notes run out

Open **Settings** and either:

- **Upgrade to Pro** – unlimited notes (plus extras like batch import and slide figures), or
- **Buy Jots** – small pay-as-you-go top-ups.

## Change the look (optional)

**Settings ▸ Appearance**: switch **Light / Dark**, and drag the **accent colour** slider to make
the whole app your colour. The pink is just the default.

## If something goes wrong

- **Nothing happens, or an error about images** → try **Generate** again. If it keeps failing, sign
  out and back in (**Settings**).
- **Can't sign in** → double-check your email and password, and that you're online.
- **Notion won't save the notes** → open **Settings ▸ Connect Notion** again and make sure you tick
  the page you want (or one of its parent pages).

---

**That's the whole thing:** slides + transcript → **Generate** → **Send to Notion**. 🎉
